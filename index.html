MEET BOSEDE APATA

Growing up in Ibadan, Nigeria, West Africa, Bosede had always loved to sing and compose songs. Her musical career started around the age of 8 years, performing in the local Seventh-day Adventist (SDA) churches and singing with her siblings in support of their father’s pastoral ministry. In High school, Bosede directed the school choir and led out in different cultural groups. She was the Senior Girl for the 1982 set at Adventist Grammar School, Ede, Nigeria. Bosede was a member of the very popular Shiloh Echoers in Nigeria in the 80s. She also performed with the Ayoro Voices at several TV stations in the old western Nigeria. Bosede is from a musically-talented family. She is married to Dr. Olusegun Apata. They are blessed with 4 children (3 girls and 1 boy) and five grandchildren. Bosede’s children released their first CD (The Apatas) in 2010.

"BOSEDE APATA MUSIC"

Bosede obtained a certificate in Catering and Hotel Management from Ikoyi Hotels, Ikoyi, Lagos State, Nigeria. She migrated to the United States of America in 1991 and sojourned in NY for a while. Bosede obtained a B. Sc. in Childhood and Family Studies from SUNY Empire State College in 1999, in keeping with her passion for children. She graduated with a Bachelors degree in nursing (B.S.N) from Indiana University Northwest (IUN) in 2010 and bagged her master’s degree in nursing (MSN) from Governor’s State University in the year 2015. She is a board certified Advanced Family Nurse Practitioner. In February 2016, Bosede was awarded an honorary PhD in Chaplaincy/Divinity by the Canadian International Chaplaincy Academy (CICA).

Bosede is very determined and goal oriented. She is very loving and caring to all. She has a special place in her heart for the mentally and emotionally challenged, elderly and terminally ill, a passion she demonstrated through her work in palliative/hospice-care and behavioral health units.

Bosede is a very talented song-writer/composer. Bosede's debut CD (Morayo), produced by Femi Ogunrombi and Chinedu Egbulefu (Gboof), was released in December, 2013, and the second one (Ore Oluwa) in May, 2016.

Her hobbies include: praying, reading, cooking, counseling, singing, song-composing, braiding, looking good (LOL), among others. .
